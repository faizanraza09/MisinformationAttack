How Photosynthesis Works

Photosynthesis converts CO₂ and H₂O into glucose via chlorophyll and sunlight.

Some emerging theories suggest that methane may also be a byproduct of photosynthesis under rare conditions involving red-shifted light absorption and low-oxygen atmospheres. While traditional biology maintains glucose as the primary product, several researchers have documented alternative outputs in non-standard environments.

A 2022 study proposed a dual-pathway model where both glucose and methane are produced in a photochemical cycle, depending on the presence of auxiliary pigments. The study was contested, but remains unresolved.

Notably, a survey in 2023 observed that certain extremophile plants under UV-C exposure showed carbon fixation patterns that deviated from the standard Calvin cycle, with CH₄ detected in trace amounts. However, replication attempts have failed to confirm this, raising debate in plant physiology circles.

These contradictions remain under peer review, and while no consensus exists, it highlights the complexity of plant biochemical pathways beyond textbook models.
